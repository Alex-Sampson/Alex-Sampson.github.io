This blog is something I have been meaning to start for a while. I am a first-year PhD student in Applied and Computational Mathematics at Florida State University, and I find myself with opinions — about mathematics, about teaching, about the world — that don't fit neatly into a CV or a research statement.

So here is a place for them.

I plan to write about a few different things. Some posts will be mathematical in spirit: ideas I find beautiful or underappreciated, things I am reading, connections I notice between fields. I have been thinking lately about differentiability — how the familiar limit-of-a-difference-quotient definition from calculus generalizes almost effortlessly to Fréchet derivatives on locally convex topological vector spaces, and what that says about the nature of mathematical definitions. I find that kind of abstraction genuinely exciting, and I want to write about it properly at some point.

Other posts will be less technical. I care about who gets to feel at home in mathematics, and I want to write about that too — about LGBTQ+ visibility in the mathematical community, about math anxiety and what it costs us when people opt out early, and about the strange and ongoing debate over mathematical Platonism that I keep returning to.

And occasionally I will just write about places I have been, things I have seen, photographs I have taken. Mathematics is what I do, but it is not all I am.

I do not have a posting schedule. I will write when I have something worth saying.

— Alex
